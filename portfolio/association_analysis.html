Introduction

Anyway, association analysis is the process of discover interesting relationships in a data set. There are two main issues that come with association analysis: one, most associations are "spurious" - they exist, but are not really meaningful or attention worthy; two, discovering patterns is expensive. There are a couple of terms that are used frequently within association analysis: itemset, k-itemset, transaction width, itemset support count. An itemset is simply a collection of items such as {Eggs, Ham, Cigarettes}. A k-itemset simply a count of how many items are in a given itemset. Transaction width is the number of items that are given within a transaction. Itemset support count means how often a given itemset appears in several supersets.

Rules

Rules were the next topic of concern. Rules are expressed as X -> Y, however the way that they're used it would be more accurate to say that X|Y - Given X, we'll probably see Y and vice versa. For example {Milk, Diapers} -> {Beer}. There are two ways of measuring rules - rule support and confidence. Rule support measures the strength of the rule and it is calculated as X u Y / N, where "u" is union. Another way to think of it is how_many_times(stuff_in_X and stuff_in_Y) / N. Rules with low support are not very interesting because they are likely a result of chance. We use rule support to eliminate uninteresting rules. Confidence, on the other hand is, how frequent the items in Y appear in X. Confidence is expressed as X u Y / Y. Confidence gives us a measure of the reliability of a rule's strength, a higher confidence implies a higher likely hood that we'll see Y given X

apriori

One of the biggest principles guiding association analysis is the principle of apriori. The apriori principle states that if we have an itemset and it's frequent, then its subset must be frequent. The inverse also applies: if we have an itemset and it is infrequent, then its supersets are infrequent as well. The apriori principle is so important because by applying it, we can significantly reduce the amount of work necessary when calculating relevant associations.
